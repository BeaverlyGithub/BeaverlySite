Things People Ask Before They Chill

We got you. Chilla is not a bank, not an investment service, not a brokerage. It’s your co-pilot for simplified market participation. And yes, your money stays with you.

What is Chilla?

+

Chilla™ is your market participation co-pilot. Think of it like a financial automation assistant powered by smart logic. Quietly working in the background within your specified instructions while you focus on life.

How do I get access?

+

Login, connect, and chill. From that point on, Chilla starts handling the automation and monitoring within your instructions in the background.

What if I don't have a broker account?

+

If you're new, you’ll need to register with a regulated broker in your country. Chilla connects to most global brokers. You choose, we adapt.

Where can I use Chilla?

+

You can chill with Chilla on any broker that supports Chilla's integration and the markets you select. Our goal? Wherever your money moves, Chilla moves with you. Effortlessly.

Can I withdraw funds anytime?

+

Yes, absolutely. Chilla doesn’t hold your capital. Your money stays in your broker account, and you have full control at all times. You can chill without feeling locked in.

Do I need trading knowledge or experience?

+

Chilla is designed for enabling market participation without requiring you to become a trader. Just connect your broker, select your instructions, and let Chilla handle the automation while you stay in the loop.

Is Chilla a bot or investment platform?

+

Chilla is your market co-pilot that helps you maintain disciplined automation not an investment platform. We never hold your money. You maintain full control of your capital through your own broker account.

Is my money safe?

+

Yes. Your funds remain in your regulated broker account. Beaverly never has custody of your money. We only provide your market co-pilot.

Does Chilla guarantee any returns?

+

No. Returns vary depending on market conditions and the logic used. Chilla focuses on disciplined automation and long-term consistency, but all markets involve risk.
