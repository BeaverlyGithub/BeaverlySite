How It Connects

Authorization, not access.

Chilla connects to your broker account through a structured, user-authorized flow. You decide the scope — Chilla operates within it.

01

You open an account with a supported broker

Open and fund an account directly with the broker of your choice. Beaverly has no involvement in this process — your account relationship is entirely between you and your chosen broker.

02

You authorize Chilla to connect

Inside Chilla, you provide authorization credentials for your environment. Chilla uses these to establish a read-and-execute connection — strictly within the scope you approve. Beaverly never stores your broker login credentials.

03

You set instructions and comfort limits

Choose a behavior, set your per-position exposure, and optionally apply a monthly comfort cap. Chilla shows you the implications before anything runs. Nothing activates without your explicit confirmation.

04

Chilla runs within your defined scope

Once authorized, Chilla observes market conditions and executes only when your instructions are satisfied. Flow surfaces every meaningful action in plain language, in real time. You can pause or revoke at any point.

Common Questions

Account compatibility, explained.

Clear answers to the questions users ask most before connecting their broker.

Does my broker need to be on this list?

Not necessarily. If your broker supports cTrader accounts, there's a strong chance Chilla can connect to it — even if it isn't listed here. The named brokers are popular examples, not an exhaustive list. During account setup, Chilla will confirm compatibility when you connect.

Is Chilla affiliated with or partnered with these brokers?

No. Beaverly is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or in a business relationship with any broker or prop firm named here. These are account environments Chilla is technically compatible with. Beaverly does not receive referral fees or commissions for broker account openings.

What happens to my broker account if I cancel Chilla?

Nothing. Your broker account is completely independent of your Chilla subscription. Cancelling Chilla simply disconnects the automation. Your account, your balance, and your ability to withdraw are unaffected.

Can Chilla withdraw money from my account?

No. Chilla has no withdrawal or transfer permissions on your account. The connection is limited to execution within your authorized instruction scope and reading account data to populate Flow. Beaverly cannot and does not touch your capital.

Why is Deriv showing as temporarily paused?

Chilla's direct Deriv integration uses a live API connection, and the underlying infrastructure is currently being migrated to improve reliability and expand capabilities. Deriv-native behaviors (Options, Multipliers, Accumulators) will return automatically when the upgrade is complete. No action is required from users with existing Deriv connections.

Can I connect multiple broker accounts to Chilla?

Yes. Chilla supports multiple environments from a single dashboard. You can connect accounts across different compatible brokers and manage instructions and comfort limits for each independently. Environment capacity depends on your Chilla plan.

Is Chilla suitable for funded prop trading accounts?

Technically, yes — if the prop firm uses cTrader. However, you are solely responsible for ensuring your use of Chilla complies with your prop firm's trading rules. Some firms restrict algorithmic or automated participation. Always verify your firm's rules before connecting a funded account. Chilla makes no representations about prop firm compliance outcomes.

Does Chilla support accounts on MT4 or MT5?

Not currently. Chilla's infrastructure is built on the cTrader ecosystem and the Deriv API. MetaTrader 4 and MetaTrader 5 account environments are not supported at this time. This may change as Chilla's account compatibility infrastructure evolves.
