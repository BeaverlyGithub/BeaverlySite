Instructions and Comfort

How to create, pause, and revoke instructions

This page covers the instruction lifecycle and the comfort controls that define your automation boundaries.

Article type: Task + Reference Last reviewed: March 12, 2026

Where to find this

  • Dashboard card: Instructions -> Tap to create instructions opens the setup wizard.
  • Dashboard card settings icon opens Your Instructions (`mandate-settings.html`).
  • Inside Your Instructions, the action bar includes Add, Update, Pause, and Revoke.

What you can do here

  • Create an instruction with selected behavior and playground context.
  • Set Comfort per position and optionally enable Omega Shield Cap.
  • Activate from wizard step 5. Activate.
  • Pause, update, add to, or revoke from Your Instructions.
  • Review detailed fields: Behaviours, Account, Shield Cap (Omega), Comfort per position, Omega enabled, Status, Active since.

Create an instruction

  1. Step 1 - Behaviours: choose your desired behaviour.
  2. Step 2 - Playgrounds: choose where the behaviour runs.
  3. Step 3 - Comfort: set Comfort per position; optionally enable Omega Shield Cap and Strict mode.
  4. Step 4 - Confirm: review disclosure text and tick the consent checkbox.
  5. Step 5 - Activate: review summary cards and activate.

Pause or revoke an instruction

Pause

Use when you want to temporarily stop automation while keeping your setup.

  • Current setup remains available for reactivation.
  • History remains available in Flow.

Revoke

Use when you want to fully stop the instruction lifecycle.

  • Revocation requires explicit confirmation in the UI.
  • You can create a new instruction afterward if needed.

Instruction FAQ

Can I change comfort after activation?

Yes. Use Update in Your Instructions and review the updated lifecycle in Flow.

Why does my instruction not move to Active?

Check environment readiness, account selection, comfort settings, and consent completion. Then review Flow and activity status for pause reasons.

Is comfort financial advice?

No. Comfort is your own user-defined boundary setting. Chilla enforces what you configure.
